CPU Scheduling

PREEMPTIVE
VS
NON-PREEMPTIVE

Preemptive Scheduling

Preemptive scheduling allows the operating system to interrupt a running process and allocate the CPU to another process for better responsiveness.

Non-Preemptive Scheduling

Non-preemptive scheduling allows a process to continue execution until completion without interruption.

Conclusion

Preemptive scheduling improves system responsiveness and is commonly used in modern operating systems. Non-preemptive scheduling is simpler and easier to implement but may result in slower response times. Both scheduling methods are important concepts in Operating Systems.

Deadlock in Operating Systems

What is Deadlock?

A deadlock is a situation in an operating system where two or more processes are unable to continue because each one is waiting for a resource held by another process. As a result, none of the processes can proceed, and the system becomes stuck.

4 Necessary Conditions

  • Mutual Exclusion
  • Hold and Wait
  • No Preemption
  • Circular Wait

How to Prevent Deadlock

Deadlocks can be prevented by breaking one of the four conditions:

  • Avoid circular waiting
  • Allow resource preemption
  • Do not hold multiple resources
  • Allow sharing of resources
